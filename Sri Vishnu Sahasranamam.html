श्रीविष्णुसहस्रनामस्तोत्रम्

Vishnu Sahasranamam

Context: This hymn is found in the Mahabharata, Anushasana Parva. It consists of 1,000 names of Lord Vishnu, revealed by Bhishma Pitamaha to Yudhishthira on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

पूर्वभागः (Introductory Prayers)

These verses are chanted to invoke the protection of the Lord and the guidance of the Sages.

शुक्लाम्बरधरं विष्णुं शशिवर्णं चतुर्भुजम् ।
प्रसन्नवदनं ध्यायेत् सर्वविघ्नोपशान्तये ॥
Meaning: We meditate upon Lord Vishnu, who is clad in white robes, who has the color of the moon, who has four arms, and who has a serene, pleasant face; may He remove all obstacles.
व्यासं वसिष्ठनप्तारं शक्तेः पौत्रमकल्मषम् ।
पराशरात्मजं वन्दे शुकतातं तपोनिधिम् ॥
Meaning: I salute Sage Vyasa, the great-grandson of Vasistha, the grandson of Shaktri, the son of Parashara, and the father of Shuka—the pure treasure of penance.
व्यासाय विष्णुरूपाय व्यासरूपाय विष्णवे ।
नमो वै ब्रह्मनिधये वासिष्ठाय नमो नमः ॥
Meaning: Salutations to Vyasa, who is a form of Vishnu, and to Vishnu, who is a form of Vyasa. I bow to the descendant of Vasistha, who is a treasure of Vedic knowledge.
अविकाराय शुद्धाय नित्याय परमात्मने ।
सदैकरूपरूपाय विष्णवे सर्वजिष्णवे ॥
Meaning: Salutations to Vishnu, who is beyond change, who is pure and eternal, who is the Supreme Soul, and who is ever-victorious over all.
किमेकं दैवतं लोके किं वाप्येकं परायणम् ।
स्तुवन्तः कं कमर्चन्तः प्राप्नुयुर्मानवाः शुभम् ॥
Meaning: (King Yudhishthira asks:) "Who is the one Deity in the world? What is the one supreme goal? By praising and worshipping whom do men attain auspiciousness and peace?"
जगत्प्रभुं देवदेवमनन्तं पुरुषोत्तमम् ।
स्तुवन्नामसहस्रेण पुरुषः सततोत्थितः ॥
Meaning: (Bhishma answers:) "By always praising the Lord of the Universe, the God of Gods, the Infinite One, and the Supreme Person through His thousand names, one transcends all worldly sorrows."

स्तोत्रम् (The Thousand Names)

The first name is Vishwa (The Universe), and the last name is Sarvapraharanayudha (He who uses everything as a weapon for protection).

Verse 1
विश्वं विष्णुर्वषट्कारो भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः ।
भूतकृद्भूतभृद्भावो भूतात्मा भूतभावनः ॥ १ ॥
Meaning: He is the Universe itself, the Pervader of all, the one invoked in sacrifices. He is the Lord of the past, present, and future. He is the Creator of all beings, the Sustainer, the Essence of all existence, and the One who causes all beings to flourish.
Verse 2
पूतात्मा परमात्मा च मुक्तानां परमा गतिः ।
अव्ययः पुरुषः साक्षी क्षेत्रज्ञोऽक्षर एव च ॥ २ ॥
Meaning: The Pure Soul, the Supreme Soul, the ultimate goal of liberated souls. He is the Imperishable, the Indwelling Spirit, the Witness of all actions, the Knower of the field (body/matter), and the Undecaying one.
Verse 3
योगो योगविदां नेता प्रधानपुरुषेश्वरः ।
नारसिंहवपुः श्रीमान् केशवः पुरुषोत्तमः ॥ ३ ॥
Meaning: The goal of Yoga, the leader of those who know Yoga, the Lord of Nature and Spirit. He who assumed the Man-Lion form, the possessor of Shri (Lakshmi), the one with beautiful hair (or slayer of Keshi), and the Supreme Person.
Verse 4
सर्वः शर्वः शिवः स्थाणुर्भूतादिर्निधिरव्ययः ।
सम्भवो भावनो भर्ता प्रभवः प्रभुिरीश्वरः ॥ ४ ॥
Meaning: He is everything, the Remover of sorrows, the Auspicious one, the Immovable, the Source of all elements, and the Inexhaustible Treasure. He manifests by His own will, is the Giver of everything, the Supporter, and the Lord of all power.
Verse 5
स्वयम्भूः शम्भुरादित्यः पुष्कराक्षो महास्वनः ।
अनादिनिधनो धाता विधाता धातुरुत्तमः ॥ ५ ॥
Meaning: Self-existent, the source of happiness, the Sun, the lotus-eyed, and the possessor of the Great Sound (Vedas). He has no beginning or end, is the Support of the universe, the Dispenser of fruits of action, and the Supreme Substratum.

उद्भवः सुन्दरः सुन्दो रत्ननाभः सुलोचनः ।
अर्को वाजसनः शृङ्गी जयन्तः सर्वविज्जयी ॥ ८५ ॥
Meaning: The one born of his own will, the beautiful, the giver of joy, the jewel-navelled, and the one with beautiful eyes. The Sun, the giver of food, the horned one, the ever-victorious, and the all-knowing victor.
Verse 86
सुवर्णबिन्दुरक्षोभ्यः सर्ववागीश्वरेश्वरः ।
महाह्रदो महागर्तो महाभूतो महानिधिः ॥ ८६ ॥
Meaning: The one with golden limbs, the unshakeable, and the Lord of the Lords of speech. The great lake (of bliss), the great abyss, the great being, and the great treasure.
Verse 87
कुमुदः कुन्दरः कुन्दः पर्जन्यः पावनोऽनिलः ।
अमृतांशोऽमृतवपुः सर्वज्ञः सर्वतोमुखः ॥ ८७ ॥
Meaning: The one who delights in the earth, the one who pierced the earth (to bring up water), the giver of pure boons, the rain-cloud, the purifier, and the air. The nectar-rayed moon, the one of immortal form, the all-knowing, and the one with faces everywhere.
Verse 88
सुलभः सुव्रतः सिद्धः शत्रुजिच्छत्रुतापनः ।
न्यग्रोधोऽदुम्बरोऽश्वत्थश्चाणूरान्ध्रनिषूदनः ॥ ८८ ॥
Meaning: The one easily attained (through devotion), the one of pure vows, the perfect, the conqueror of enemies, and the scorcher of foes. The banyan tree, the fig tree, the holy peepal tree, and the slayer of the wrestler Chanura.
Verse 89
सहस्रार्चिः सप्तजिह्वः सप्तैधाः सप्तवाहनः ।
अमूर्तिरनिघोऽचिन्त्यो भयकृद्भयनाशनः ॥ ८९ ॥
Meaning: The one with a thousand rays, the seven-tongued fire, the seven fuels, and the one with seven horses (the Sun). The formless, the flawless, the unthinkable, the creator of fear (for the wicked), and the destroyer of fear (for the devotees).
Verse 90
अणुर्बृहत्कृशः स्थूलो गुणभृन्निर्गुणो महान् ।
अधृतः स्वधृतः स्वास्यः प्राग्वंशो वंशवर्धनः ॥ ९० ॥
Meaning: The atomic, the massive, the lean, the stout, the bearer of attributes, the attribute-less, and the great. The un-supported, the self-supported, the beautiful-faced, the first of dynasties, and the multiplier of the dynasty.
Verse 91
भारभृत्कथितो योगी योगीशः सर्वकामदः ।
आश्रमः श्रमणः क्षामः सुपर्णो वायुवाहनः ॥ ९१ ॥
Meaning: The bearer of the world's weight, the one praised by all, the king of Yogis, and the fulfiller of all desires. The resting-place, the one who purifies the diligent, the one who wanes (during Pralaya), the beautifully-winged, and the one for whom the wind is a vehicle.
Verse 92
धनुर्धरो धनुर्वेदो दण्डो दमयिता दमः ।
अपराजितः सर्वसहो नियन्ताऽनियमोऽयमः ॥ ९२ ॥
Meaning: The wielder of the bow, the science of archery, the scepter of punishment, the punisher, and the punishment itself. The undefeated, the one who bears everything, the controller, the one who is not controlled, and the one who is deathless.
Verse 93
सत्त्ववान् सात्त्विकः सत्यः सत्यधर्मपरायणः ।
अभिप्रायः प्रियार्होऽर्हः प्रियकृत्प्रीतिवर्धनः ॥ ९३ ॥
Meaning: The one full of purity, the virtuous, the truth, and the one devoted to truth and dharma. The goal of all, the one worthy of love, the worshipful, the doer of good, and the increaser of joy.
Verse 94
विहायसगतिर्ज्योतिः सुरुचिर्हुतभुग्विभुः ।
रविर्विरोचनः सूर्यः सविता रविलोचनः ॥ ९४ ॥
Meaning: The one who moves in space, the light, the beautiful splendor, the consumer of offerings, and the all-pervading. The sun, the illuminator, the source of light, the creator, and the one whose eye is the sun.
Verse 95
अनन्तो हुतभुग्भोक्ता सुखदो नैकजोऽग्रजः ।
अनिर्विण्णः सदामर्षी लोकाधिष्ठानमद्भुतः ॥ ९५ ॥
Meaning: The infinite, the consumer of sacrifices, the enjoyer, the giver of happiness, the one born many times, and the first-born. The untiring, the one who forgives the virtuous, the foundation of the world, and the wonderful.
Verse 96
सनात्सनातनतमः कपिलः कपिरव्ययः ।
स्वस्तिदः स्वस्तिकृत्स्वस्ति स्वस्तिभुक्स्वस्तिदक्षिणः ॥ ९६ ॥
Meaning: The beginningless, the most ancient, the sage Kapila, the sun/boar, and the inexhaustible. The giver of well-being, the maker of well-being, well-being itself, the enjoyer of well-being, and the distributor of well-being.
Verse 97
अरौद्रः कुण्डली चक्री विक्रम्यूर्जितशासनः ।
शब्दातिगः शब्दसहः शिशिरः शर्वरीकरः ॥ ९७ ॥
Meaning: The one without anger, the wearer of earrings, the wielder of the discus, the valiant, and the one of firm commands. The one beyond sound, the one who bears all sounds, the cool, and the creator of darkness (night).
Verse 98
अक्रूरः पेशलो दक्षो दक्षिणः क्षमिणां वरः ।
विद्वत्तमो वीतभयः पुण्यश्रवणकीर्तनः ॥ ९८ ॥
Meaning: The non-cruel, the charming, the skillful, the efficient, and the best among the patient. The most learned, the one free from fear, and the one whose names are holy to hear and chant.
Verse 99
उत्तारणो दुष्कृतिहा पुण्यो दुःस्वप्ननाशनः ।
वीरहा रक्षणः सन्तो जीवनः पर्यवस्थितः ॥ ९९ ॥
Meaning: The one who lifts us out of existence, the destroyer of evil deeds, the holy, and the destroyer of bad dreams. The slayer of demons, the protector, the virtuous, the life-force, and the all-pervading.
Verse 100
अनन्तरूपोऽनन्तश्रीर्जितमन्युर्भयापहः ।
चतुरश्रोगभीरात्मा विदिशो व्यादिशो दिशः ॥ १०० ॥
Meaning: The one of infinite forms, infinite glory, the conqueror of anger, and the remover of fear. The one of perfect form, the deep soul, and the one who pervades all directions.
Verse 101
अनादिर्भूर्भुवो लक्ष्मीः सुवीरो रुचिराङ्गदः ।
जननो जनजन्मादिर्भीमो भीमपराक्रमः ॥ १०१ ॥
Meaning: The Beginningless, the support of the Earth, the wealth of the world, the great Hero, and the wearer of beautiful armlets. The Creator of all, the root cause of birth, the Terrible (to evil), and the one of formidable prowess.
Verse 102
आधारनिलयोऽधाता पुष्पहासः प्रजागरः ।
ऊर्ध्वगः सत्पथाचारः प्राणदः प्रणवः पणः ॥ १०२ ॥
Meaning: The fundamental Support, the one who needs no support, the one whose smile is like a blooming flower, and the ever-vigilant. The one who moves upward (beyond matter), the follower of the path of truth, the giver of life, the sacred Om, and the worthiest of all.
Verse 103
प्रमाणं प्राणनिलयः प्राणभृत्प्राणजीवनः ।
तत्त्वं तत्त्वविदेकात्मा जन्ममृत्युजरातिगः ॥ १०३ ॥
Meaning: The ultimate Authority, the resting place of life, the nourisher of life, and the life-force itself. The Truth, the knower of truth, the one Soul, and the one who transcends birth, death, and old age.
Verse 104
भूर्भुवःस्वस्तरुस्तारः सविता प्रपितामहः ।
यज्ञो यज्ञपतिर्यज्वा यज्ञाङ्गो यज्ञवाहनः ॥ १०४ ॥
Meaning: The tree of the three worlds (Bhur, Bhuvah, Svah), the one who helps cross existence, the Creator, and the Great-Grandfather (of Brahma). The Sacrifice, the Lord of sacrifice, the Performer, the limb of sacrifice, and the vehicle of sacrifice.
Verse 105
यज्ञभृद्यज्ञकृद्यज्ञी यज्ञभुग्यज्ञसाधनः ।
यज्ञान्तकृद्यज्ञगुह्यमन्नमन्नाद एव च ॥ १०५ ॥
Meaning: The sustainer of sacrifice, the creator of sacrifice, the lord of sacrifice, the consumer of offerings, and the means of sacrifice. The one who concludes the sacrifice, the secret of sacrifice, the Food, and the Eater of food.
Verse 106
आत्मयोनिः स्वयञ्जातो वैखानः सामगायनः ।
देवकीनन्दनः स्रष्टा क्षितीशः पापनाशनः ॥ १०६ ॥
Meaning: The source of the self, the self-born, the excavator of the universe, and the singer of Samas. The joy of Devaki (Krishna), the Creator, the Lord of the Earth, and the Destroyer of sins.
Verse 107
शङ्खभृन्नन्दकी चक्री शार्ङ्गधन्वा गदाधरः ।
रथाङ्गपाणिरक्षोभ्यः सर्वप्रहरणायुधः ॥ १०७ ॥
Meaning: The wielder of the Conch, the Sword (Nandaka), the Discus, the Bow (Sharnga), and the Mace. The one whose hand is the chariot-wheel, the unshakeable, and the one who uses every element as a weapon for protection.
Verse 108
वनमाली गदी शार्ङ्गी शङ्खी चक्री च नन्दकी ।
श्रीमान्नारायणो विष्णुर्वासुदेवोऽभिरक्षतु ॥ १०८ ॥
Meaning: Adorned with forest garlands, holding the mace, the bow, the conch, the discus, and the sword; may the illustrious Narayana, Vishnu, Vasudeva protect us all.

नमः सर्वहितार्थाय जगदाधारहेतवे ।
साष्टाङ्गोऽयं प्रणामस्ते प्रयत्नेन मया कृतः ॥

Salutations to the Lord, the benefactor of all, the cause and support of the world. I offer this prostration with all my heart.

॥ इति श्रीमहाभारते अनुशासनपर्वणि श्रीविष्णुसहस्रनामस्तोत्रं सम्पूर्णम् ॥

Thus ends the Shri Vishnu Sahasranamam Stotram found in the Mahabharata.

फलश्रुतिः (The Benefits of Recitation)

The Phalashruti (the "listening to the fruits") describes the spiritual, mental, and physical benefits of reciting or hearing the thousand names of Vishnu. It serves as the conclusion to the stotram in the Mahabharata.

फलश्रुतिः (The Benefits of Recitation)

The following verses describe the merits gained by those who engage with this hymn with devotion.

इतीदं कीर्तनीयस्य केशवस्य महात्मनः ।
नाम्नां सहस्रं दिव्यानामशेषेण प्रकीर्तितम् ॥
Meaning: Thus, the thousand divine names of the great soul Kesava, who is worthy of all praise, have been sung in their entirety.
य इदं शृणुयान्नित्यं यश्चापि परिकीर्तयेत् ।
नाशुभं प्राप्नुयात् किञ्चित्सोऽमुत्रेह च मानवः ॥
Meaning: That man who hears this daily or recites it shall never meet with anything inauspicious, either in this world or the next.
वेदान्तगो ब्राह्मणः स्यात् क्षत्रियो विजयी भवेत् ।
वैश्यो धनसमृद्धः स्यात् शूद्रः सुखमवाप्नुयात् ॥
Meaning: The seeker of knowledge will gain wisdom; the warrior will gain victory; the businessman will gain wealth; and the worker will gain happiness. (Representing that all paths of life are blessed).
धर्मार्थी प्राप्नुयाद्धर्मं अर्थार्थी चार्थमाप्नुयात् ।
कामानवाप्नुयात्कामी प्रजार्थी प्राप्नुयात्प्रजाम् ॥
Meaning: He who seeks righteousness (Dharma) will find it; he who seeks wealth will gain it; he who seeks pleasures will enjoy them; and he who desires progeny will be blessed with children.
न वासुदेवभक्तानामशुभं विद्यते क्वचित् ।
जन्ममृत्युजराव्याधिभयं नैवोपजायते ॥
Meaning: No evil ever befalls the devotees of Vasudeva. They are freed from the fear of birth, death, old age, and disease.
कायेन वाचा मनसेन्द्रियैर्वा बुद्ध्यात्मना वा प्रकृतेः स्वभावात् ।
करोमि यद्यत्सकलं परस्मै नारायणायेति समर्पयामि ॥
Universal Dedication:
Whatever I perform with my body, speech, mind, senses, intellect, or soul, or by my innate nature—I dedicate all of it to the Supreme Lord Narayana.

॥ ओम् तत् सत् ॥
